Build vs Buy

This page helps executive teams compare O2 partnership against in-house development, using the same dimensions CFO and CTO use for final approval.

Decision matrix

Score each model from 1 to 5. Use weighted scoring where 5 means highest priority.

Time to value
  • Expected pilot go-live in weeks
  • Dependency on internal hiring
  • Readiness of existing integrations
Total cost profile
  • 12-24 month TCO forecast
  • CAPEX vs OPEX split
  • Cost of model operations and governance
Risk and accountability
  • Security and compliance burden
  • Production support ownership
  • Risk of timeline overrun
Strategic control
  • Roadmap flexibility
  • IP and custom logic ownership
  • Ability to scale across use cases

When O2-led delivery wins

  • You need measurable impact in under one quarter.
  • Your team lacks production AI orchestration experience.
  • Executive priority is lower delivery risk and faster payback.
  • You want managed operations with governance from day one.

When in-house build can make sense

  • You already run a mature AI platform team in production.
  • Your use cases require deep product-specific custom IP.
  • You can fund a longer runway before value realization.
  • You accept higher near-term execution risk for long-term control.
