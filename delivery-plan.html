Delivery Plan

A practical framework for launching EVA and Virtual Buddy on the B2B market, scaling delivery, and running the solution as a repeatable O2 business capability.

B2B market launch and scaling model

The solution should enter the B2B market as a productized service, not as a one-off custom project. O2 should sell a repeatable evaluation-to-pilot-to-scale motion with clear packaging, reusable assets, and governance that protects delivery quality as demand grows.

1. Productize the offer
  • Define EVA and Virtual Buddy packages, add-ons, and entry criteria
  • Create standard proposal, security, architecture, and business case templates
  • Separate MVP, scale, and managed-operation commercial motions
2. Qualify B2B opportunities
  • Score customers by volume, use-case clarity, API readiness, and executive sponsorship
  • Use discovery workshops to confirm pain, value, feasibility, and risk
  • Route opportunities into standard pilot, custom delivery, or advisory track
3. Launch controlled pilots
  • Start with high-volume, low-ambiguity use cases and measurable KPIs
  • Limit scope, traffic, and channels until quality gates are met
  • Use pilot outcomes to prove ROI and create referenceable success stories
4. Build a scale factory
  • Reuse playbooks, prompt patterns, test scenarios, runbooks, and governance artifacts
  • Standardize handover from sales to delivery to operations
  • Track delivery capacity, margin, customer outcomes, and roadmap demand

Delivery model

EVA delivery should run as a joint product stream: O2 provides the orchestration platform, implementation expertise, and delivery governance, while the client owns business outcomes, source-system readiness, and final operational acceptance.

1. Discovery and framing
  • Prioritize use cases by value, complexity, and risk
  • Confirm channels, customer journeys, and fallback paths
  • Define KPIs, acceptance criteria, and target pilot scope
2. Solution and dependency design
  • Map data entities, APIs, IAM, audit, and logging needs
  • Agree integration pattern and non-functional constraints
  • Prepare backlog, estimates, environments, and test data
3. Build, validate, and operationalize
  • Configure EVA flows, prompts, rules, and integrations
  • Run functional, security, UAT, and regression validation
  • Prepare monitoring, runbooks, support model, and training
4. Pilot, scale, and govern
  • Launch controlled pilot with agreed traffic and segments
  • Review KPIs, incidents, containment, and customer feedback
  • Approve expansion roadmap and steady-state governance

Operating model for scale

After pilot success, EVA and Virtual Buddy need a steady operating model covering ownership, quality, incident handling, optimization, and commercial expansion. The goal is to keep the platform reliable while continuously adding value across customers and use cases.

Product governance
  • Own product roadmap, packaging, pricing inputs, and reusable capabilities
  • Maintain decision rules for what becomes standard vs. custom
  • Review portfolio KPIs, customer feedback, and delivery bottlenecks monthly
Delivery capability
  • Run discovery, solution design, implementation, testing, and go-live readiness
  • Maintain reusable templates for workshops, estimates, integrations, and UAT
  • Manage partner or specialist capacity when demand exceeds internal teams
Operations and quality
  • Monitor SLA, containment, latency, escalation rate, and response quality
  • Operate incident process, model tuning, SARA review, and customer reporting
  • Operate AI testing as a paid add-on for regression checks, response validation, and release readiness
  • Run quarterly optimization plans with each customer
Commercial scaling
  • Enable sales teams with qualification scripts, demo journeys, and objection handling
  • Convert pilots into expansion roadmap and managed-operation contracts
  • Separate billing for platform subscription, LLM usage, connectors, testing, and custom development
  • Build vertical playbooks once patterns repeat across customers

Recommended O2 capability model

Core roles
  • B2B product owner for EVA and Virtual Buddy
  • Solution architect and integration lead
  • AI conversation designer and prompt/test specialist
  • Connector and knowledge-ingestion specialist
  • Delivery manager and customer success manager
  • Operations lead for monitoring, incidents, and optimization
Operating cadence
  • Weekly delivery steering during active implementations
  • Weekly operations review during pilot and first month after go-live
  • Monthly product and commercial portfolio review
  • Quarterly customer value review and expansion planning

30-60-90 day structure

Days 0-30: Scope and design
  • Finalize priority use cases
  • Confirm data and API dependencies
  • Define governance and escalation rules
Days 31-60: Build and validate
  • Implement workflows and integrations
  • Run QA, security checks, and UAT
  • Train operations and business owners
Days 61-90: Pilot and optimize
  • Controlled pilot release
  • KPI tracking and tuning cycles
  • Scale recommendation and executive review
Beyond 90 days: Scale
  • Expand channels and use cases
  • Operationalize governance and reporting
  • Transition to steady-state model

Orientational milestones

The dates below are planning anchors, not contractual commitments. Final timing depends on API readiness, security review, test data availability, and the number of use cases in scope.

Week 0 Kick-off and decision owners confirmed

Scope, stakeholders, governance cadence, and escalation path are agreed.

Weeks 1-2 Use-case shortlist and KPI baseline

Priority journeys, target metrics, and initial business case assumptions are locked.

Weeks 3-4 Solution blueprint signed off

Architecture, integrations, IAM, logging, fallback, and compliance needs are validated.

Weeks 5-8 MVP flows and integrations ready for UAT

Core EVA journeys are implemented with test data, monitoring, and handover notes.

Weeks 9-10 UAT, security validation, and runbooks complete

Business users, security owners, and operations confirm launch readiness.

Weeks 11-12 Controlled pilot go-live

Limited audience or traffic share is routed to EVA with daily monitoring and tuning.

Weeks 13-14 Pilot review and scale decision

KPIs, defects, escalations, and customer feedback drive the rollout recommendation.

RACI starter (fill in owners)

  • Business owner: KPI definition and success criteria
  • Architecture lead: technical blueprint and constraints
  • Security owner: control validation and approval
  • Operations owner: monitoring, incident process, runbooks
  • Delivery manager: timeline, dependencies, escalation handling

Go-live gates

  • Functional and integration acceptance complete
  • Security controls signed off by relevant stakeholders
  • Support processes and fallback paths tested
  • Executive sign-off on pilot KPIs and risk posture
